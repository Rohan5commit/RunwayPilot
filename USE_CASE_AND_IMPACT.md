# Use Case And Impact

## Startup founder use case

A founder uploads six months of transactions before a fundraising update. RunwayPilot shows that revenue has softened over the last three months, infrastructure spend has spiked, and a duplicated software charge is still active. The founder runs a downside scenario with revenue down 20 percent and immediately sees runway compress by more than a month. That allows earlier action instead of reactive cuts later.

## Small business use case

A small business owner without a finance team imports transaction data to understand why cash feels tighter despite stable top-line performance. RunwayPilot identifies rising payroll burden, recurring SaaS drag, and one unusually large vendor bill. The owner gets a practical set of actions rather than a pile of raw transactions.

## Incubator or accelerator use case

An accelerator can use RunwayPilot as a lightweight portfolio review tool. Demo-ready ingestion and explainable risk findings make it easier to identify which teams need financial coaching earlier. It is especially useful for student founders and first-time operators who do not yet have disciplined reporting habits.

## Measurable impact

- surface runway compression earlier than monthly spreadsheet reviews
- reduce duplicated software and recurring waste before it compounds
- shorten scenario-planning time from hours to minutes
- improve the quality of founder updates with clearer risk framing
- enable more disciplined spending decisions before the company enters emergency mode
