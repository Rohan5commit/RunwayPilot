# Presentation Script

Start with the problem.

Every startup says runway matters, but most teams still manage it through stale spreadsheets and fragmented finance data. That means they notice problems after the damage is already real: burn creeps up, revenue softens, infrastructure spikes, duplicate tools stay active, and nobody sees the compression early enough to act.

That is the gap RunwayPilot closes.

RunwayPilot is an AI finance copilot for startups and small businesses. You upload a CSV, or just load our demo data, and in seconds you get an operating picture that is actually usable: cash balance, monthly burn, runway, expense trends, anomalies, vendor concentration, and scenario forecasting.

Here is the core demo flow.

First, we open the homepage and load a sample startup dataset. Immediately, the dashboard shows runway, burn, revenue trend, expense trend, largest cost category, and the highest-risk alert. This is not just a chart wall. It is a decision surface.

Next, we reveal a real problem in the data. RunwayPilot catches an unusual infrastructure spike and a duplicated software subscription. Then the AI CFO panel explains, in plain business language, why runway has tightened and what the team should investigate first.

Under the hood, the product is built as five agents. The Intake Agent cleans the data. The Classification Agent maps spend into meaningful business buckets. The Forecast Agent calculates burn and projects runway across baseline, optimistic, and conservative cases. The Risk Agent detects anomalies, concentration, recurring waste, and cashflow deterioration. Finally, the Strategy Agent uses NVIDIA NIM to turn those findings into structured recommendations.

Then we simulate a downside case, like revenue down 20 percent. The runway chart updates instantly, the risk level increases, and the AI layer refreshes the recommended actions. That is the moment the value clicks: this is not accounting software, and it is not a generic chatbot. It is a startup operating tool.

We finish by exporting a founder-ready report that summarizes the metrics, the risks, and the next actions.

RunwayPilot fits Orion because it scores across all four criteria equally. It is innovative because it combines deterministic financial reasoning with explainable AI. It presents clearly, it functions end-to-end, and it solves a painful real-world problem for founders, SMBs, and accelerators.

Cash problems should be visible before they become existential. RunwayPilot makes that visible early enough to matter.
